# Target Audience Analysis

## Primary Audiences

### 1. Musicians Practicing Instrument Technique

**Demographics**:
- Age: 12-65
- All skill levels (beginner to professional)
- Instruments: Guitar, piano, drums, bass, violin, wind instruments
- 85% practice alone, need reliable tempo reference

**Needs**:
- Accurate, consistent tempo
- Variable BPM for speed building
- Time signature flexibility
- Subdivision options for rhythm precision
- Distraction-free interface

**Pain Points**:
- Physical metronomes are expensive ($30-100+)
- Apps require downloads and often subscriptions
- Google metronome too basic for serious practice
- Need different sounds for different instruments

**Use Case Example**:
> "I'm learning a Bach piece at 60 BPM and need to gradually work up to 120 BPM over the next month. I also need to practice the triplet sections separately."

**Features They Value Most**:
1. Tempo trainer (gradual increase)
2. Subdivisions (especially triplets)
3. Clear visual beat indicator
4. Keyboard shortcuts (hands on instrument)
5. Setlist for multiple pieces

---

### 2. Music Students (School/University/Private Lessons)

**Demographics**:
- Age: 8-25
- Enrolled in formal music education
- Practice assignments with specific tempo requirements
- Often use school computers or shared devices

**Needs**:
- Quick access without installation
- Matches tempo markings from sheet music
- Time signatures beyond 4/4
- Parent-friendly (no scary permissions)
- Works on Chromebooks and school devices

**Pain Points**:
- Can't install apps on school computers
- Parents hesitant about paid subscriptions
- Need to match exact tempos from assignments
- Limited understanding of tempo terminology

**Use Case Example**:
> "My teacher said practice this piece at Andante (92 BPM) in 6/8 time. I need to practice 30 minutes daily and show I did."

**Features They Value Most**:
1. Tempo marking presets (Largo, Andante, etc.)
2. Educational explanations
3. Practice timer with session history
4. No account required
5. Works on any device

---

### 3. Drummers and Percussionists

**Demographics**:
- Age: 14-45
- Hobbyist to professional
- Often practice in loud environments
- Need precise timing for fills and transitions

**Needs**:
- High accuracy (drummers notice millisecond variations)
- Strong visual indicator (can't always hear in loud room)
- Complex time signatures (5/4, 7/8, odd meters)
- Polyrhythm practice support
- Accent patterns for groove development

**Pain Points**:
- Generic metronomes don't handle odd times well
- Need to develop internal timing independent of click
- Expensive pro solutions (Drumeo, etc.)
- Transitioning between songs at different tempos

**Use Case Example**:
> "I'm working on a 7/8 pattern with accents on 1 and 4. I need to practice with the click, then gradually remove it to test my internal timing."

**Features They Value Most**:
1. Odd time signatures (5/4, 7/8, 9/8)
2. Customizable accent patterns
3. Muted beats training
4. Polyrhythm support (3:2, 4:3)
5. Very clear visual indicator

---

### 4. Dance and Exercise Enthusiasts

**Demographics**:
- Age: 18-55
- Fitness instructors, dancers, runners
- Need consistent BPM for choreography or pace
- Often use during movement (can't adjust easily)

**Needs**:
- Specific BPM matching (workout playlists, dance routines)
- Large, visible display
- Simple controls (start/stop while moving)
- Different sounds for different activities
- Works on phone/tablet at gym

**Pain Points**:
- Need specific BPM for different exercises
- Can't easily adjust while in motion
- Want fullscreen for visibility
- May not understand musical terminology

**Use Case Example**:
> "I'm teaching a spin class and need exactly 140 BPM for the hill climb section. I need a big display I can see across the room."

**Workout BPM Guide**:
| Activity | BPM Range |
|----------|-----------|
| Yoga/Stretching | 60-90 |
| Walking | 100-120 |
| Jogging | 120-140 |
| Running | 140-160 |
| High-Intensity | 150-180+ |
| Zumba/Dance | 130-170 |

**Features They Value Most**:
1. Fullscreen mode
2. Large BPM display
3. Quick BPM presets for common workout tempos
4. Simple start/stop
5. Tap tempo for matching existing music

---

### 5. Music Teachers and Instructors

**Demographics**:
- Age: 25-65
- Private lesson teachers, school music teachers
- Teaching multiple students at various levels
- Need demonstration tools

**Needs**:
- Quick switching between settings for different students
- Visual demonstration of rhythm concepts
- Educational content to share with students
- Works on classroom smartboard/projector
- Save settings for different students/pieces

**Pain Points**:
- Limited classroom technology budgets
- Need to explain rhythm concepts visually
- Different students at different levels
- Can't remember each student's current tempo goals

**Use Case Example**:
> "Student A is working on Minuet at 100 BPM in 3/4, Student B is doing scales at 80 BPM in 4/4. I need to switch quickly between them and track their progress."

**Features They Value Most**:
1. Setlist with multiple saved configurations
2. Educational content for teaching
3. Clear visual representation of beats
4. Time signature explanations
5. Progress tracking per student (future)

---

### 6. Songwriters and Producers (Secondary)

**Demographics**:
- Age: 18-45
- Home studio musicians
- Creating music with DAWs
- Need tempo reference during composition

**Needs**:
- Quick BPM check during writing
- Tap tempo to find song's natural BPM
- Reference for common genre tempos
- Eventually: MIDI sync capability

**Pain Points**:
- DAW metronomes sometimes limited
- Need standalone reference while away from computer
- Want to know BPM of reference tracks

**Use Case Example**:
> "I'm writing a house track and want to check if 126 or 128 BPM feels better before committing in Ableton."

**Features They Value Most**:
1. Tap tempo
2. Genre BPM reference guide
3. Quick, accurate playback
4. Clean sound that doesn't interfere with composition

## User Journey Maps

### New Musician (Beginner)
1. Google searches "free metronome online"
2. Finds our site, immediately sees clean interface
3. Clicks "Start" - metronome plays at default 120 BPM
4. Adjusts tempo using slider to match sheet music
5. Reads tempo guide to understand "Andante" marking
6. Practices for 20 minutes
7. Returns tomorrow, appreciates same settings preserved

### Serious Student (Intermediate)
1. Teacher assigns piece at specific tempo
2. Opens our metronome, sets exact BPM and time signature
3. Starts practice timer for 30-minute session
4. Uses tempo trainer to gradually increase from 80 to 100 BPM
5. Saves this piece to setlist for next session
6. Shows parent the practice timer history

### Professional Drummer (Advanced)
1. Needs to learn complex 7/8 pattern for studio session
2. Sets time signature, creates custom accent pattern
3. Uses muted beats mode to test internal timing
4. Works on 3:2 polyrhythm section
5. Saves multiple songs from the upcoming session to setlist
6. Uses fullscreen on iPad on music stand

### Fitness Instructor
1. Needs 140 BPM click for spin class
2. Opens site on gym tablet
3. Taps tempo button to find exact BPM of inspiration track
4. Enters fullscreen mode
5. Large display visible across room
6. Simple start/stop control

## Key Insights

### 1. Accessibility is Critical
- Must work without download or account
- Must work on all devices (phone, tablet, Chromebook, desktop)
- Must work in school/library/gym environments

### 2. Depth Without Complexity
- Simple interface for quick use
- Advanced features discoverable but not overwhelming
- Educational content available but not required

### 3. Practice-Focused, Not Just Tempo-Focused
- Users want to improve, not just hear clicks
- Timer, trainer, and tracking features matter
- Setlist enables structured practice

### 4. Visual Feedback Matters
- Drummers in loud rooms need visual
- Teachers demonstrating need clear display
- Fitness users need large, distant-readable display

### 5. Educational Content is Differentiator
- Students need context for tempo markings
- Teachers need shareable explanations
- Beginners need guidance on practice techniques

Serves: All musicians, students, drummers

### Phase 2: Advanced Training
Serves: Drummers, advanced musicians
- Muted beats training
- Custom accent patterns

### Phase 3: Education & Tracking
Serves: Students, teachers
